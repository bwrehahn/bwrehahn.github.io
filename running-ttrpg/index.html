Welcome Future Game Masters!

Have you ever wanted to become a Dungeon Master?  What about a storyteller?  This site is dedicated to helping you learn how to run a Pencil and Paper Role-Playing Game Commonly known as a Table top RPG. Whether you have been a player of these RPG's for years or just wanting to get started in this hobby or just wanting more information, this guide will give you the basics for setting up and running your own game. This is important because for every 20 players there is only 1 game master and that leads to crowding in games, or people getting left out. It is this way because many people are afraid it may be too much work or they may not be any good at it. This website's goal is to show it that while this may be a bit time consuming, it isn't as hard as it may seem, and with these basics you and your friends can create adventures and stories you will remember for a long time.

The navigation menu at top goes in order of topics you should cover from left to right, so you want to go to common knowledge before you go to planning the game, and if this is your first time going through the site you should not skip ahead, but if you are a returning user you can just go to the information you need to look up.

 

What this Site Covers

This site has several sections that can be reached from the above navigation menu. Here is a breakdown of what each section contains:

  1. Home - That's this screen with the welcome and explanations.
  2. Common Knowledge - This area covers basic information that is used and can be applied to most RPG's.
  3. Planning the Game - This section is probably the longest, and that's because the more you plan, the easier running the game will be. This has three subsections:
    1. Pre-Planning -This is mostly logistical stuff to see if you and your friends can play the game or even if you want to. If you can't get any of the information in this section together, the game will be doomed before it is started and there is no reason to go on.
    2. Preparation -This section covers finalizing ideas and making preparations for the games, such as planning events, missions, goals, etc.
  4. Playing the Game - This section is for information you can use while playing the game. It covers player character creation, things to know while running the game, and some helpful hints and advice to keep in mind while running the game.
  5. After the Game - Just because you are done for the night doesn't mean your work is done. This section covers verifying information and getting feedback from other players.
  6. Tips for Success - This has some final advice you can use while running the game.
  7. Additional Resources - This section contains links to other resources you can use for being a game master and an e-mail form that you can use for suggestions and comments on the website to make this site better.
