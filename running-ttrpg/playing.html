Playing the Game

Now comes the fun part! This section covers character creation and actually playing the game. This is what all your work has lead to.

Character Creation

The first night of the game includes the Players creating their characters. It is considered playing and not preparation because it occurs on the first night everyone can get together and it is usually followed by a short sequence of play that brings all the Players and their Characters together in the story and shows how all the Characters met This is optional, but a lot of players like it. For example, if you decide to play a medieval fantasy story, a good place for all the characters to meet is during a bar fight in the local tavern. It also introduces them to the basic rules. This part is optional on the first night of play. It is possible they can create a character before arriving on the night of the first game, but it is a good idea that all Players are together when doing this because they can also bounce ideas off of each other when creating their own character and possibly make one they would enjoy more.

The rules for creating characters vary from game to game but would be listed in your Game book. If not everybody has a copy be sure to share and pass around, although this does make the process longer. You may consider making copies of these pages to pass around for reference.

Make yourself available when the Players are creating their characters in case the Players have questions or concerns. You can also offer advice to the Players if you see a way they can improve, but do not force them to take your advice. No one like being told to play THEIR CHARACTER THEY WAY YOU WANT THEM TO PLAY IT!

Running the Game

Whether or not you played the same night as Character creation or not, this is where the fun really starts. All the preplanning, planning, preparation, and brand new Player Characters are ready to go. Here is what you need to keep in mind.

1. Relax - If this is your first time being a Game Master, you will probably be nervous, but don't be. You've done everything you can to this point and if something goes wrong, you should be familiar enough with the rules of the game you are playing in order to adjust. And plus, you are among friends, and they should forgive the occasional slip-up.

2. Have Fun - This is why everybody who plays these games does this. Why would you be doing this if you were not having fun? Make sure everyone else is having fun too. Is someone isn't having fun, they will be a downer and cause everybody else to either not have as much fun or they will ruin all your hard work.

3. Rules in Play - As the Game Master, if your Players are unfamiliar with the rules, such as it is their first time playing, you need to guide them along as to what the rules are, and you can do this as the situation comes up, such as in the first fight the players get into with a bad guy, you can explain how the rules work and then they can learn as they play at the same time.

4. Be Flexible - This was covered in the Preparation phase but it bears repeating. You can't anticipate everything the Players will do. For example, if part of your story is to have the characters stop travel caravan of bad guys in medieval fantasy game, you may expect them to ambush the caravan, but if one of them decides to befriend the caravan by pretending to be mentally handicapped and then kill the bad guys when they don't expect it, you will have to adjust.

5. House Rules - Another thing to consider while being flexible is something called "House Rules". These are rules that you and your group have agreed to follow that are not necessarily in the game book. If there is a rule in the Game book that nobody likes, you can just ignore it, or change it to be something else. For example, if you are playing a science fiction game and there is a rule that states they need to charge their laser swords every half an hour and nobody likes this, you can all agree to change the rule to say the players need to charge their laser swords only once a day, or you can disregard the rule altogether and make it so they don't have to recharge their laser swords at all.

Keep these in mind and you will surely have a good time.
