After the Game

This is the phase that takes place after the game. It covers verifying the Players had fun and an additional work you may have to do.

Verify the Players had fun

This part is obvious. You need to verify the other Players had fun. This was covered in the last section and it is repeated here because that is the most important part. If the Players did not have fun, you need to do something different.

Ask for Feedback

This also goes back to being flexible, Ask the players if how they think you did being their Game Master and what they liked and did not like. For this part you need to be open to criticism. Do not get defensive but be open with them, and they will be open with you. For example, the Players may have thought the game was too hard, so you could do some things and implement some "House Rules" to make it easier.

You may also have to change your plan and story slightly if something really bad happened that needs to change.

Also take note of what the Players liked and build on that. For example, if you created a villain for the player to fight that they all really liked, you can bring that villain back next time, or if the group had a challenging puzzle they had to solve, you can include more of those in future games.
